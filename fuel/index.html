Source assumptions

Default carb targets are duration-based and set to practical starting points from sports nutrition consensus: very short sessions can be 0 g/h, then roughly 30 g/h, 60 g/h, and up to 90 g/h as duration increases and tolerance allows.

These are defaults only. Override carbs/h to match your gut training, intensity, and race conditions.

Method

Creating your mix

Most ingredients naturally mix well.

  • For best results, place all ingredients in a blender and blend for 30s.
  • Otherwise:
    • If using epsom salts, use a ziplock bag or pestle and mortar to grind into finer particles.
    • Place ingredients into an airtight container or ziplock bag and shake vigorously.

Once stored, it is easiest to store in large tubs or jars.

Creating Gels

  1. Use the Planner tab to calculate required carbohydrates.
  2. Dry mix (g) = target carbs / 0.875.
  3. Water (g/ml) = dry mix * (40/60).
  4. Gel ratio is 60% mix and 40% water.
  5. Mix boiling water with specified carbohydrate mix.
  6. (Optional) add hydration mix.
  7. Use a mini-whisk or fork to create a smooth but viscous liquid.
  8. Pour directly into gel containers.
  9. Refrigerate before usage.

Creating Sports Drinks

  1. Use the planner to calculate how much carbohydrate mix you need for your drink.
  2. Place the dry mix into a glass container (e.g. measuring jug).
  3. Add a small quantity of boiling water - this helps the mixture dissolve.
  4. (Optional) add hydration mix.
  5. Top up to your desired bottle size with cold water.
  6. Refrigerate before usage.

Creating Hydration Drinks

We drink one of these daily to help with overall hydration, even when not exercising. The solution is almost-palatable with just water, but you may find it easier to drink with flavouring. We would recommend a natural cordial.

  1. Add 4.8g (approx 1/2 Tbsp) of mix into a glass.
  2. Add 350ml water and mix thoroughly.
  3. (Optional) add flavourings of your choice.
